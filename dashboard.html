Global Warming Effects

—— A rise in surface tempearture, natural disasters and sea level.

Rising Surface Tempearture

The world is getting warmer.

According to an ongoing temperature analysis conducted by scientists at NASA, the average global temperature on Earth has increased by a little more than 1° Celsius (2° Fahrenheit) since Industrial Revolution. Preponderance of evidence saying humans are mostly responsible.

Rising Natural Disasters

The charts show the 5 most frequent natural disasters rising trend by year.

With increasing global surface temperatures the possibility of more droughts and increased intensity of storms will likely occur. As more water vapor is evaporated into the atmosphere it becomes fuel for more powerful storms to develop. More heat in the atmosphere and warmer ocean surface temperatures can lead to increased wind speeds in tropical storms.

Rising Sea Level

Melting glaciers under global warming make sea level rising.

When averaged over all of the world’s oceans, absolute sea level has risen at an average rate of 0.06 inches per year from 1880 to 2013. Since 1993, however, average sea level has risen at a rate of 0.12 to 0.14 inches per year—roughly twice as fast as the long-term trend.

Rising sea level can cause land loss and submergence. These are all terrible chain reactions by global warming that we should not ignore.
